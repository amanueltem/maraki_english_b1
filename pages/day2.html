Day 2

Looking Back: Talking About the Past

Learn how to talk about past events, experiences, and activities using proper grammar and expressions.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Use simple past tense correctly
  • Talk about past habits with "used to"
  • Use expressions for reflecting on the past

Simple Past Tense

Regular Verbs: Add -ed

Positive

"I cleaned my room yesterday."

"She worked late last night."

Negative

"I didn't clean my room."

"She didn't work late."

Question

"Did you clean your room?"

"Did she work late?"

Irregular Verbs: Different Forms

Positive

"I had lunch at 1 PM."

"We went to the park."

Negative

"I didn't have lunch."

"We didn't go to the park."

Question

"Did you have lunch?"

"Did they go to the park?"

Common Irregular Verbs:

go → went

eat → ate

see → saw

take → took

make → made

come → came

do → did

get → got

think → thought

run → ran

speak → spoke

write → wrote

"Used To" for Past Habits

Positive

"I used to play soccer when I was younger."

"She used to live in London."

Negative

"I didn't use to play tennis."

"He didn't use to like coffee."

Question

"Did you use to play an instrument?"

"Did they use to live here?"

When to Use "Used To":

  • For past actions or activities we no longer do
  • For past states or situations that have changed
  • For past habits that are different from present habits

Expressions for Talking About the Past

Good old days

A nostalgic reference to a better time in the past

Example:

"In the good old days, we played outside until dark."

Back in the day

Referring to a time in the past, often with nostalgia

Example:

"Back in the day, we didn't have smartphones."

Back then

Referring to a specific time in the past

Example:

"Back then, I lived in a small apartment."

Now that I think about it

When you realize something after reflection

Example:

"Now that I think about it, that was a bad idea."

In hindsight

Understanding a situation only after it has happened

Example:

"In hindsight, I should have studied more."

It was all for the best

Saying that something turned out well despite difficulties

Example:

"Losing that job was hard, but it was all for the best."

Past Encounters & Discoveries

Run into / Ran into

To meet someone unexpectedly

Examples:

"I ran into my old teacher at the supermarket."

"We often run into each other at the gym."

Come across

To find or discover something by chance

Examples:

"I came across some old photos while cleaning."

"She comes across interesting articles online."

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Complete the Sentences

Fill in the blanks with the correct past tense form of the verb.

1

Yesterday, I __________ (clean) my entire apartment.

2

She __________ (not/have) time for lunch yesterday.

3

__________ you __________ (run into) anyone interesting last week?

4

I __________ (use to play) tennis every weekend when I was younger.

Exercise 2: Write About Your Past

Write a short paragraph about your past experiences using the grammar and expressions we learned.

Try to use at least 3 expressions and both regular and irregular past tense verbs
