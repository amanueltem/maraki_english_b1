Day 7

Modals and Deduction

Master modal verbs to express ability, possibility, permission, requests, advice, obligation, and deduction.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Use modal verbs to express different functions
  • Make deductions using modal verbs
  • Ask polite questions and make requests
  • Connect ideas using linking words

Can - Ability, Possibility, Requests

Ability

"I can swim very well."

"She can speak three languages."

"They can't dance salsa."

Requests & Permission

"Can you help me with this?"

"Can I borrow your pen?"

"You can use my computer."

Possibility & Deduction

"She can get a ride from her brother."

"You can't be serious!"

"That can't be right."

Could - Past Ability, Polite Requests, Possibility

Past Ability

"I could dance well when I was younger."

"She could speak French fluently."

"I couldn't hear you."

Polite Requests

"Could you show me the way?"

"Could I have a glass of water?"

"Could you please repeat that?"

Possibility & Suggestions

"He could come to the party."

"We could watch a movie."

"It could rain later."

Should - Advice & Suggestions

Advice & Suggestions

"You should go to the doctor."

"We should leave early to avoid traffic."

"He should study more for the exam."

Regret & Criticism

"I should have known better."

"You shouldn't have said that."

"She should have called me."

May & Might - Possibility & Permission

Permission

"May I leave early today?"

"May I use your phone?"

"You may go now."

Possibility

"She might be late."

"He might not know about the meeting."

"It may rain tomorrow."

Must & Have to - Obligation & Necessity

Obligation

"You must wear a seat belt."

"Students must complete all assignments."

"You have to study for the test."

Necessity

"I'm thirsty. I need to drink water."

"She has to finish her work."

"We need to buy groceries."

Linking Words

Adding Information

"Besides English, she speaks French."

"In addition to his job, he volunteers."

"She's smart. Moreover, she's hardworking."

"I like coffee. I also like tea."

Contrast & Cause/Effect

"I wanted to go, but I was tired."

"It's expensive. However, it's good quality."

"It was raining, so we stayed home."

"He studied hard. Therefore, he passed."

"She was late. As a result, she missed the bus."

Emphasis & Condition

"I love languages. In fact, I'm learning three."

"It was difficult. Indeed, it was the hardest test."

"We'll go unless it rains."

"Study hard; otherwise, you might fail."

Dialogue: Using Modals in Conversation

Scenario: Maria and Tom are planning their weekend

Maria:

What should we do this weekend? I can go out on Saturday, but I have to study on Sunday.

Tom:

We could go hiking. The weather might be nice. However, it may rain, so we should have a backup plan.

Maria:

Could you check the weather forecast? Besides hiking, we could go to the museum. I've heard it's interesting.

Tom:

That's a good idea. We must buy tickets online first. Otherwise, we might not get in.

Maria:

You can't be serious! Do we really need to book in advance?

Tom:

Yes, in fact, it's usually sold out on weekends. Therefore, we should book now.

Maria:

Okay, may I use your laptop to book the tickets? As a result of your suggestion, we'll have a great weekend!

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Modal

Select the appropriate modal verb for each sentence.

1. I ________ speak Spanish when I was younger.

2. You ________ wear a helmet when riding a bike.

3. ________ I borrow your pen, please?

4. It ________ rain later, so take an umbrella.

Exercise 2: Complete with Linking Words

Fill in the blanks with appropriate linking words.

Sentence 1: "I like coffee. ________, I prefer tea."

Sentence 2: "She studied hard. ________, she passed the exam."

Sentence 3: "We'll have a picnic ________ it doesn't rain."

Exercise 3: Role Play Scenarios

Practice these conversations with a partner using modals and linking words.

Scenario 1: Asking for Permission

Person A: You want to leave work early to go to a doctor's appointment.

Person B: You are the boss. Your employee is asking to leave early.

Use these modals:

  • "May I...?"
  • "Could I...?"
  • "Can I...?"

Scenario 2: Giving Advice

Person A: You're feeling sick but have an important meeting.

Person B: Your friend is sick. Give them advice about what to do.

Use these modals:

  • "You should..."
  • "You could..."
  • "You might want to..."
