Day 1

My Typical Day

Learn vocabulary and grammar to describe your daily routines and activities.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Use vocabulary related to daily routines
  • Apply simple present tense correctly
  • Describe your typical day in English

Daily Routine Vocabulary

Go ahead / Head out

To leave or start going somewhere

Example:

"I usually head out for work at 8 AM."

Commute

To travel regularly between home and work

Example:

"I commute to work by train every day."

Clock in / Clock out

To record the time you start/finish work

Example:

"Employees must clock in when they arrive."

Unwind / Wind down

To relax after a period of work or tension

Example:

"I like to wind down with a book in the evening."

Break the ice

To make people feel more comfortable in social situations

Example:

"I told a joke to break the ice at the meeting."

Grab a bite

To get something to eat quickly

Example:

"Let's grab a bite before the movie."

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense

When to Use Simple Present Tense

Routines & Repeated Actions

"I go to work every day."

"She exercises in the morning."

Facts & Truths

"The sun rises in the east."

"Water boils at 100Â°C."

Opinions & Feelings

"I like coffee in the morning."

"He thinks English is difficult."

Scheduled Events

"The train leaves at 5 PM."

"Class starts at 9 AM."

Simple Present Tense Forms

Subject Positive Negative Question
I / You / We / They I go I don't go Do I go?
He / She / It He goes He doesn't go Does he go?

Important Notes:

  • Add -s or -es for he/she/it
  • Use do/does for questions and negatives
  • Time expressions: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day

Examples:

  • "I don't like waking up early."
  • "Does she work on weekends?"
  • "He doesn't commute by car."
