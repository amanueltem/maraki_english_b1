Day 3

Storytelling & Investigation Questions

Learn to tell detailed stories and handle investigation questions with confidence.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Tell detailed stories about daily routines
  • Understand and respond to investigation questions
  • Use proper tenses and vocabulary for storytelling
  • Participate in role-play conversations

Sara's Daily Routine Story

A Productive Day

Reading and comprehension exercise

Every morning, Sara wakes up early because she wants to keep a strong routine. She makes her bed, drinks a glass of warm water, and goes outside to take a short walk. While walking, she listens to motivational podcasts because they help her stay focused throughout the day.

After the walk, Sara returns home and prepares a healthy breakfast. She usually eats oatmeal with fruit, but sometimes she tries new recipes to improve her cooking skills. Then, she starts her work. She works from home as an online consultant, so she spends a lot of time speaking with clients on video calls.

In the afternoon, she takes a break to read books about psychology and personal growth. She believes learning something new every day keeps the mind strong. In the evening, she relaxes, writes in her journal, and plans goals for the next day.

Grammar Focus:

  • Present simple tense for routines and habits
  • Time expressions: every morning, usually, sometimes, in the afternoon, in the evening
  • Conjunctions: because, so, but, while

General Investigation Question Bank

Timeline & Location

  • Where were you yesterday?
  • What time did you arrive there?
  • Who was with you?
  • How long did you stay?

Actions

  • What exactly did you do first?
  • What did you do after that?
  • Did you leave at any point? Why?
  • What were you doing when the incident happened?

Witnesses

  • Who saw you?
  • Did anyone talk to you?
  • Did anyone give you anything?
  • Did you see anyone acting strange?

Objects / Evidence

  • Did you touch that item?
  • Did you carry a bag or anything?
  • Did you take anything home with you?

Drama & Pressure Questions

Motives

  • Why did you go there in the first place?
  • Did something happen that made you angry?
  • Did you need money or something valuable?

Suspicion Pressure

  • Why do three people say they saw you near the scene?
  • Why did you leave so suddenly?
  • Why didn't you tell anyone where you went?

Consistency Check

  • Can you repeat your timeline again?
  • Are you sure your story didn't change?
  • Does your story match what the others said?

Heat-Up Questions

  • If you're innocent, why do you look nervous?
  • Are you hiding something?
  • What part of your story doesn't make sense even to you?

Conversation Activities

Role Play Activities (1-3)

1) The Yesterday Debrief

Role A: You talk about what happened yesterday.

Role B: You are the manager asking for details.

  • • How did your day start?
  • • What did you do in the afternoon?
  • • What problems did you face?
  • • How did the day end?

2) The Lost Item

Role A: You lost something yesterday.

Role B: You help ask questions to find it.

  • • When did you last see it?
  • • Where did you go after that?
  • • Who were you with?
  • • What did you do to try to find it?

3) Restaurant Problem

Role A: You had a bad restaurant experience yesterday.

Role B: You are the restaurant manager listening.

  • • What happened?
  • • How did the staff treat you?
  • • What were you expecting instead?
  • • What solution do you want now?

Discussion Activities (4-5)

4) The Trip Story

Both partners talk. Each person tells a short story about a trip in the past.

Ask each other:

  • • Where did you go?
  • • What did you enjoy?
  • • What went wrong?
  • • Would you go again? Why or why not?

5) I Used to Believe…

Both partners talk. Share something you believed in the past that changed later.

Ask each other:

  • • What did you believe before?
  • • What experience changed your mind?
  • • How did you feel during that time?
  • • What do you think now?

Your Output Instructions

  • Speak only in past tense
  • Keep the conversation natural
  • Ask follow-up questions (don't just answer once and stop)
