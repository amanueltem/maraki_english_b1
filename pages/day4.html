Day 4

Time Will Tell: Future Tenses

Learn to talk about future plans, decisions, and predictions using different future forms.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Use "will" for spontaneous decisions and promises
  • Use "going to" for planned future actions
  • Use "about to" for immediate future events
  • Participate in free conversation using different tenses

"Will" - Spontaneous Decisions & Promises

Sudden Decisions

"I'll call you later."

"I won't stay long."

Used when deciding at the moment of speaking

Promises & Threats

"I will always love you."

"I'll never forget this."

Used for commitments and warnings

Special Expressions:

"If it's God's will, I will..."

Expressing hope dependent on external factors

"Will you marry me?"

A life-changing spontaneous question

"Where there's a will, there's a way"

Motivational saying about determination

"Will" as permission

"Will you let me borrow your book?"

"Going To" - Planned Future

Planned Actions

"I'm going to travel the world."

"She's going to study medicine."

Used for intentions and plans made before speaking

Predictions with Evidence

"Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."

"He's going to be late - he left 30 minutes ago."

Used when we can see evidence for the future event

Common Questions:

  • "What are you going to do tomorrow?"
  • "When are you going to eat?"
  • "Where are you going to live next year?"

"About To" - Immediate Future

Very Near Future

"We're about to have a free talk."

"I'm about to leave the house."

Used for actions that will happen very soon

Running Out of Time

"Hurry! The movie is about to start."

"We're about to run out of time."

Used when something is going to happen imminently

Free Talk: All Tenses Practice

Simple Present (Everyday habits, facts, routines)

1. Daily Routine

  • • What do you usually do after work/school?
  • • Why do you prefer that routine?
  • • If you could change one part of your day, what would it be?

2. Food Habits

  • • What kind of food do you eat most often?
  • • Why do you choose that food?
  • • Do you cook it yourself or buy it?

3. Relationships

  • • Who do you spend most of your time with?
  • • What do you usually do together?
  • • How does this person influence your life?

4. Stress Management

  • • How do you manage stress in your daily life?
  • • Does your method actually work?
  • • What method should you be using instead?

Simple Past (Memories, experiences, stories)

6. Travel Memories

  • • Where did you go on your last trip?
  • • What was the best part of the trip?
  • • What would you do differently if you went again?

7. Childhood Memory

  • • What was your favorite memory from childhood?
  • • Why does this memory stand out?
  • • Do you think that experience shaped who you are now?

8. Important Lesson

  • • Talk about a time when you learned something important.
  • • Who or what taught you the lesson?
  • • Did it change the way you think or behave?

9. Proud Moment

  • • When was the last time you felt proud of yourself?
  • • What did you do to feel that way?
  • • Who did you share the moment with?

Simple Future (Plans, hopes, predictions)

11. Next Month Focus

  • • What is one thing you will focus on next month?
  • • Why is this your focus?
  • • What could stop you from achieving it?

12. Future Living

  • • Where do you think you will live in the future?
  • • Why do you see yourself there?
  • • What kind of lifestyle do you imagine for yourself?

13. Learning Goals

  • • What skill will you try to learn next?
  • • Why that skill specifically?
  • • How will you practice it consistently?

14. Future Relationships

  • • Who will you spend more time with next year?
  • • Why do you want to invest in that relationship?
  • • What will change in your daily life because of it?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Complete with Future Forms

Choose the correct future form (will, going to, or about to) for each sentence.

1

I __________ (call) you when I get home.

2

Look at those dark clouds! It __________ (rain).

3

Hurry up! The movie __________ (start).

Exercise 2: Write About Your Future Plans

Write a short paragraph about your future plans using different future forms.

Try to use 'will', 'going to', and 'about to' in your paragraph
