Day 6

Small Problems, Simple Solutions

Learn to talk about daily inconveniences and offer practical suggestions using common English phrases.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Describe common daily problems and inconveniences
  • Use suggestion phrases to offer solutions
  • Discuss practical ways to solve everyday issues
  • Participate in conversations about problem-solving

Common Daily Problems

Technology Issues

  • "My internet is so slow today."
  • "My phone battery dies too quickly."
  • "My computer keeps freezing."

Time Management

  • "I always wake up late for work."
  • "I oversleep almost every morning."
  • "I'm always rushing everywhere."

Transportation Issues

  • "There was a heavy traffic jam."
  • "My car broke down on the way to work."
  • "The police pulled me over for speeding."

Money & Personal Issues

  • "I spend too much money on eating out."
  • "I lost my keys again."
  • "I spilled coffee on my shirt and now there's a stain."

Suggestion Phrases

Polite Suggestions

  • "You could try..."

    "You could try setting multiple alarms."

  • "Why don't you...?"

    "Why don't you take a different route to work?"

  • "Have you thought about...?"

    "Have you thought about creating a budget?"

Direct Suggestions

  • "You should..."

    "You should call a mechanic to check your car."

  • "It might help if you..."

    "It might help if you restart your router."

  • "How about...?"

    "How about packing your bag the night before?"

Responding to Suggestions:

Positive Responses

  • "That's a good idea!"
  • "I'll try that, thanks."
  • "Why didn't I think of that?"

Hesitant Responses

  • "I'm not sure that would work."
  • "I've tried that before."
  • "That might be difficult for me."

Practical Solutions

Technology Problems

Slow Internet

  • • Restart your router
  • • Limit devices connected to Wi-Fi
  • • Contact your internet provider

Phone Battery Dies Quickly

  • • Reduce screen brightness
  • • Close unused apps
  • • Carry a portable charger

Time Management

Always Waking Up Late

  • • Set multiple alarms
  • • Place alarm across the room
  • • Go to bed earlier

Oversleeping

  • • Establish a consistent sleep schedule
  • • Avoid screens before bed
  • • Use a sunrise alarm clock

Transportation Issues

Heavy Traffic Jams

  • • Leave earlier or later to avoid rush hour
  • • Use navigation apps to find alternative routes
  • • Consider public transportation

Car Broke Down

  • • Call roadside assistance
  • • Have emergency contacts saved
  • • Keep your car regularly maintained

Money & Personal Issues

Spending Too Much Money

  • • Create a weekly budget
  • • Cook at home more often
  • • Use cash instead of cards

Spilled Coffee / Stains

  • • Immediately rinse with cold water
  • • Use stain remover
  • • Keep a stain removal pen at work

Dialogue: Problem-Solving Conversation

Scenario: Maria is complaining to her friend Tom about her daily problems

Maria:

I'm having such a bad day! First, I overslept and was late for work.

Tom:

Oh no! Why don't you set multiple alarms? That usually helps me.

Maria:

I tried that, but I just turn them off and go back to sleep. Then, when I was rushing to work, I got stuck in a terrible traffic jam!

Tom:

You could try leaving 30 minutes earlier. The traffic is much lighter then.

Maria:

But if I leave earlier, I'd have to wake up even earlier! And to make things worse, I spilled coffee on my white blouse and now there's a huge stain.

Tom:

Have you thought about keeping a spare shirt at work? I always do that for emergencies.

Maria:

That's actually a good idea. I should do that. And my internet at home has been so slow lately too.

Tom:

You should contact your internet provider. It might be a problem with your connection.

Maria:

Thanks for all the suggestions, Tom. I feel better already!

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Match Problems with Solutions

Match each problem with the most appropriate solution.

1. I always wake up late for work.

2. My internet is very slow.

3. I spend too much money eating out.

4. There's always heavy traffic on my way to work.

Exercise 3: Role Play Scenarios

Practice these problem-solving conversations with a partner.

Scenario 1: Car Problems

Person A: Your car broke down on the way to an important meeting. You're telling your friend about it.

Person B: Your friend is complaining about their car problems. Offer helpful suggestions.

Use these phrases:

  • "You could try calling..."
  • "Why don't you...?"
  • "Have you thought about...?"

Scenario 2: Financial Issues

Person A: You're spending too much money each month and can't save anything.

Person B: Your friend is worried about their finances. Offer practical suggestions.

Use these phrases:

  • "You should create..."
  • "It might help if you..."
  • "How about...?"
